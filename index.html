Serving as a judge is the honor of a lifetime.

Each day I start well before the sun comes up with a prayer for wisdom. A judge has to discern between a violent predator, and someone who is simply struggling with poverty or mental health challenges.

It is humbling work, which I thrive on. At two in the morning, I may get phone calls from social workers and law-enforcement in the field, and we must decide whether to remove children from awful homes. At five in the afternoon, I may sentence a drug dealer, a child molester, or a murderer. It is not work for the faint of heart. It is not work you do without vast experience.

It is not work that you leave at the office.

I am proud of the innovative work we have done. Our Family Preservation Court is a model for the rest of the state. Our Best for Baby program has saved children, and created better outcomes for their parents. My unmatched experience, over 500 jury trials, ensures the public safety that all of us treasure in St Tammany and Washington Parish.

I am guided in this work by my faith. I ask for your support and your prayers as I continue this work as your Judge in Division G.
